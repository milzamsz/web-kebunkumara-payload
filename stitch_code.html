Plant Profile

Common Name
Swiss Cheese Plant, Fruit Salad Plant
Scientific Name
Monstera deliciosa
Family
Araceae
Origin
Native to tropical forests of southern Mexico, south to Panama. It has become a mildly invasive species in Hawaii, Seychelles, Ascension Island and the Society Islands.

Ecological Role

Contribution to the Micro-Ecosystem

In its natural habitat, Monstera deliciosa plays a crucial role as a hemiepiphyte. Starting its life on the dark forest floor, it climbs towards the canopy, creating a bridge between the ground and the trees.

Within the context of our urban garden, this plant serves as a significant air purifier. NASA studies have listed species within the Araceae family as effective at removing toxins such as formaldehyde from the air. Furthermore, its large leaves contribute to local humidity regulation through transpiration, creating a cooler microclimate which is beneficial for surrounding delicate understory ferns.

The "Swiss cheese" holes (fenestrations) are not just aesthetic; they are an evolutionary adaptation allowing the plant to withstand heavy tropical downpours without leaf damage and letting light pass through to its lower leaves.
